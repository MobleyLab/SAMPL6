Method:
The alchemical free energy calculations were performed using the Free Energy Perturbation method that is implemented in the NAMD package. First, each replica was minimized for 100000 simulation steps using a conjugate gradient and line search algorithm. Then, each system was equilibrated for 1ns under an NPT ensemble (298.15 K and 1 atm) using a 1fs timestep. NAMD provides constant pressure simulation using a modified Nosé–Hoover method in which Langevin dynamics is used to control fluctuations in the barostat. The Langevin piston utilized an oscillation period of 100fs and a damping time scale of 50 fs.Temperature control was achieved by use of Langevin dynamics, with the Langevin coupling coefficient being 1.0 ps -1. Long range electrostatic interactions were treated with the following PME parameters: PME tolerance = 10^(-6), PME spline order 4 and PME grid = 48x48x48. The cutoff for both van der Waals and electrostatic interactions was 10 Å, and the switching distance was set to 9 Å. 

NAMD makes use of the dual-topology approach. As the MD simulation progresses, the potential energy function characteristic of λ = 0 is scaled into that representative of λ = 1. The NAMD protocol utilized a total number of 32 equidistant lambda windows, that are simulated independently for 20ns/window and a 2fs timestep. In each window, 1M of equilibration steps are performed before ensemble averaging is initiated. For the CB8-G3 system, the electrostatic interactions are linearly decoupled from the simulation over the lambda value range of 0 - 0.9. As far as the OA host-guest systems is concerned, we decouple the electrostatic interactions linearly over the lambda value range of 0 - 0.5. For all systems, the van der Waals interactions are linearly decoupled from the simulation for lambda values between 0 and 1. During the complex leg of the simulation a harmonic restraint with a wall constant of 100 kcal/mol/Å^2 was applied to prevent the guest from drifting away from the host.
